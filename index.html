About the Town

Nepal's Doorway on the Eastern Frontier

Kakarbhitta (also written Kakarvitta) sits in Mechinagar Municipality, Ward No. 10, at the eastern end of the 922 km Mahendra (East–West) Highway. Facing Panitanki in West Bengal, India, across the Mechi River, it is Nepal's busiest eastern land border and the main entry point for travelers, traders, and trekkers heading into Nepal's eastern hills. The name itself comes from local words for "land of crabs" — a nod to the wetlands the town was built on.

See & Do

Landmarks Around Town

Kakarbhitta isn't about sightseeing sprees — it's a working border town. These are the spots that give you its real character.

Landmark

Mechi Bridge

The international bridge over the Mechi River — the exact line where Nepal ends and India begins. Sunrise and late-afternoon river views are worth the walk.

Street Life

Main Bazaar

The market at the junction is where locals trade, barter, and gossip — spices, ready-made clothes, cheap electronics, and cross-border goods of every kind.

Border

Integrated Check Post

Watch trucks, traders and passport queues at Nepal's busiest eastern customs point — a front-row seat to everyday Nepal–India trade.

Highway

Eastern Terminus, Mahendra Highway

The marker noting the end of Nepal's coast-to-coast East–West Highway — a small, low-key photo stop with a big story behind it.

Schools of Kakarbhitta

A growing town needs strong schools — these are the well-known institutions serving Kakarbhitta's students with quality education.

School

One of the established secondary schools in Kakarbhitta, offering quality education and serving generations of students. Located at Mechinagar-6, the school is known for its academic excellence and student development programs.

A well-regarded boarding school offering English-medium education with a focus on holistic development. The school provides excellent facilities including green spaces and modern learning environments for students.

School

Tarapunja English School

A local English-medium school catering to families in and around Tarapunja and Kakarbhitta. Known for its academic programs and commitment to student excellence, established with a strong foundation of values and learning.

Suryodya English School

A well-known English-medium school serving the growing student population of Kakarbhitta. The school is committed to providing quality education focused on the pursuit of knowledge and holistic student development.

Good To Know

Before You Go

A Tourism Information Center at the border issues trekking permits for Nepal's eastern region. Nepali and Indian nationals cross freely; other nationalities use the customs checkpoint. Buses to Birtamod, Damak, Biratnagar and beyond leave regularly from the Kakarbhitta bus park. Best season to visit: October–April.
